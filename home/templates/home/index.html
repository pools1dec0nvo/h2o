Why this?

Urban water systems are fragile in times of crisis

Modern cities rely on centralized water infrastructure that can fail rapidly under stress. Geopolitical conflict, seismic activity, climate change, and biological contamination events increasingly threaten the reliability of potable water distribution.

In emergency scenarios, access to safe drinking water within the first 72 hours is critical for public health, safety, and social stability. Yet recent global events show that urban systems are not prepared for disruption at this scale.

Conflict Wars and sabotage can disable treatment plants and pipelines — seen in Ukraine (2022–present) and the Siege of Sarajevo
Seismic Risk Earthquakes can collapse underground networks — Kobe (1995) and Christchurch (2011) left millions without water
Extreme Drought Climate-driven water scarcity has pushed cities like Cape Town, São Paulo, and California to the brink of supply collapse
Contamination Biological and chemical crises — Flint (lead contamination) and Walkerton (E. coli outbreak) — show how quickly public water becomes unsafe
