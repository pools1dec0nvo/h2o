Stages of operation

Our system is permanently useful:

Normalcy

The system hasn't been activated yet, however, we must still ensure:

  • Strategic placement of nodes, ensuring maximum coverage and redundancy
  • Continuous monitoring of water level and quality and battery levels, with automated grid top-ups when necessary and trained maintenance personnel
  • Availability of web portal and up-to-date, public status information
  • Ease of access and transport for firefighting crews

Deployment

Disaster has struck, so the system must be activated in critical time:

  • Water levels and quality continue to be monitored
  • Activation of alternative comms protocols, if cellular network goes offline, allowing for uninterrupted monitoring
  • Transition of energy source to battery backups and solar
  • Notion of available and unavailable nodes

Catastrophe

Until access to water is restored, guarantee these:

  • Secure access to water and tamper-proof tanks
  • Dynamic volume rationing, depending on local and individual needs
  • Critical information and status updates transmitted to authorities, independently of internet access
  • Persistent modularity of transport from less affected to more affected areas
